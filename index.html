Who We Are

From conflict to community-led resilience.

Founded in 2022, CCMO was born from a simple but urgent truth: while national peace agreements exist, local conflicts driven by resource competition, cattle raiding, and revenge killings continue to fracture communities. We exist to bridge that gap — with peacebuilding, education, and access to clean water at the heart of our mission.

Our integrated approach combines peacebuilding, education, WASH, humanitarian assistance, and sustainable development — because lasting peace requires dignity, knowledge, and the most fundamental resources.

What We Do

Integrated programs, lasting change.

Our work spans interconnected program areas — each designed to reinforce the others, moving communities from immediate vulnerability toward sustained self-reliance.

Education & Literacy

Our Flagship Program

Education is the foundation of lasting peace.

In communities fractured by conflict, education does more than teach — it restores hope, builds social cohesion, and equips the next generation with the tools to break cycles of violence. CCMO's education program spans early childhood to adult literacy, with a special focus on girls' education and inclusive learning environments.

Stories from the Field

Peace begins when communities are heard — and given the space to heal, together.

In Unity State, CCMO facilitated dialogue between communities divided by decades of conflict. Through mediation forums, peace education in schools, and WASH infrastructure that serves as a shared community resource, we helped establish lasting peace — reducing revenge attacks and livestock raiding by 65% in six months.
