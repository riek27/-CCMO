Featured Story
Featured Story

Peace begins when communities are heard — and given the space to heal, together.

In Unity State, two communities had been locked in a cycle of cattle raiding and revenge attacks for over a decade. CCMO facilitated a series of dialogues — bringing elders, youth leaders, women, and local authorities to the same table. Over six months, trust was rebuilt. Today, the communities share water points, trade peacefully, and have established a joint peace committee. Revenge attacks have dropped by 65%.

A
Achol M.Community leader · Bentiu

Peace

Community Peace Dialogues Restore Trust

Through CCMO's peace program, over 200 community members from rival clans participated in facilitated dialogues. Agreements on resource-sharing and conflict resolution have led to a 60% reduction in inter-communal violence over the past year.

March 2026
Education

Accelerated Learning Brings Hope to Out‑of‑School Children

CCMO's education program has reached 500 children who never attended school or dropped out. Using accelerated learning methods, children aged 8–14 are now catching up on core subjects and preparing for formal schooling.

February 2026
WASH

Clean Water and Sanitation Transform Health

Our WASH program has drilled 15 boreholes and trained 50 community hygiene promoters. Diarrheal diseases have dropped by 45% in target villages, and handwashing with soap has become a daily norm.

January 2026
Sanitation

Launch of Community‑Led Toilet Building Initiative

CCMO has launched a new program to construct 500 household latrines in remote areas. The initiative, in partnership with local masons, promotes open‑defecation‑free communities and improves dignity for women and girls.

December 2025
Teacher Training

Empowering Teachers, Transforming Classrooms

Through intensive training, 120 primary school teachers have gained new skills in child‑centered pedagogy, classroom management, and inclusive education. Student engagement has soared, and dropout rates are falling.

November 2025
Education

CCMO Launches Education Program in Bentiu — 300 Children Enrolled

In a major milestone, CCMO has officially opened a new learning center in Bentiu, enrolling 300 children — half of them girls. With six classrooms and trained teachers, the center provides quality primary education in a region long affected by conflict and displacement.
