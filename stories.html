Unity State · Featured Story
Featured Story

Peace begins when communities are heard — and given the space to heal, together.

In Unity State, two communities had been locked in a cycle of cattle raiding and revenge attacks for over a decade. CCMO facilitated a series of dialogues — bringing elders, youth leaders, women, and local authorities to the same table. Over six months, trust was rebuilt. Today, the communities share water points, trade peacefully, and have established a joint peace committee. Revenge attacks have dropped by 65%.

A
Achol M.Community leader · Bentiu

Nyagua's Return to School

After dropping out at age 10 due to conflict and poverty, Nyagua enrolled in a CCMO-supported school at 14. Today, she is top of her class and dreams of becoming a teacher. "Education gave me back my future," she says.

WASH
Lakes State

Clean Water Transforms a Village

Before CCMO drilled a borehole, women walked 6km daily for water. Now, with clean water at the village center, waterborne illness has dropped, children attend school, and women have time for livelihood activities.
